  4. ASCE Model

    Action-gate residual MAPPO for person-priority signal control

    Agents

    12 signal agents observe local queue, speed, phase, and time-of-day.

    Policy Net

    Action-Gate Residual MAPPO with binary follow-or-override MP gate and phase head. Shared 2-layer ReLU MLP, 128 hidden.

    Critic

    Training-only centralized critic, global observation dimension 309, with per-feature Welford normalization.

    Run Setup

    5 s decisions, 900 s episodes, over 200 episodes, 8 parallel libsumo workers, 3.4x speedup.

    Reward

    Person-weighted per-person delay with local fairness. Objective is person-time-loss.

    Targets and Status
    ASCE Target at most 0.90x 0.897x
    PIRA MAPE Target At most 10 percent
    Runtime Target At most 5 s
    ASCE beats Max-Pressure by 10.3% on person-time-loss with action-gate residual MAPPO.
    PIRA GAT is implemented. Baseline datasets are in Parquet.
    ~2 GPU-hours for 200-episode parallel training on RTX 4070.

  6. Key Technical Decisions

    Action-gate residual with person-weighted reward closed the gap

    Action-Gate Residual

    Override gate learned selectively: gate fraction rose from 1.4% to 22%.

    Person-Weighted Reward

    Per-person delay weights bus at 30 and car at 1.3. Local approach fairness replaced global throughput. Objective is person-time-loss.

    Curriculum Training

    Round-robin over 4 scenarios, AM, PM, surge, midday multimodal, with 8 parallel SUMO workers. Training is active.

    Reward Evolution
    v1 Objective (interim) 7,101 s
    v3 Person-Objective 387,264 s
    Max-Pressure 431,590 s
    Removing throughput was critical. It dominated gradients and encouraged proxy gaming over delay reduction.
    Gate-fraction trend confirms selective overrides, not random exploration.
    The 4-scenario curriculum tests generalization beyond AM peak, where the target was first achieved.
  7. Reference Design (Not in Final Control Loop)

    PIRA surrogate for fast corridor what-if planning

    • Fast disruption what-if layer without full SUMO reruns.
    • Input is scenario context and per-intersection traffic state.
    • Output is corridor impact estimate and signal timing recommendations.
    none construction lane_closure transit_line major_event
    Graph Input (scenario, time_step) graph snapshots with intersections as nodes
    Backbone Node MLP (128), 3 GATConv layers with 4 heads, residual LayerNorm
    Dual Heads Impact head for delay, throughput, queue_total. Timing head for green_ns_s and green_ew_s.
    Required Inputs
    • Requires osm.net.xml, dataset.parquet, and a trained checkpoint.
    • intersection_map.csv is optional.
    Features and Topology
    • 17 node features: 10 traffic, 7 scenario.
    • Capacity set to 0 removes affected edges.
    Training Protocol
    • MSE with Adam (1e-3), gradient clip 1.0.
    • Default curriculum, early stop patience 15, split by scenario_id.
    Proposal Targets
    • MAPE at most 10 percent and R^2 at least 0.85.
    • Inference under 5 s per scenario.
