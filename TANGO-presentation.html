  3. ASCE Architecture

    MAPPO with shared policy and centralized critic

    Agents

    8 signalized intersections in the current benchmark, each acting from local observations.

    Policy Net

    2-layer ReLU MLP, 128 hidden units, parameter sharing across all intersections.

    Critic

    Centralized critic sees concatenated observations during training only.

    Run Setup

    5s decision interval, episodes up to 300s, 30 training episodes, 10-seed evaluation.

    Success Criteria and Status
    ASCE Target <= 0.90x
    PIRA MAPE <= 10%
    PIRA Runtime < 5s
    ASCE is running end to end and beats Fixed-Time in interim tests.
    PIRA is planned but not yet implemented in the interim report.
    Reported ASCE compute budget is under 1 GPU-hour on RTX 4070 Laptop GPU.

  5. Why Max-Pressure Still Leads

    Current benchmark is a nominal demand regime

    Benchmark Regime

    70 calibrated car flows in a mostly stationary demand setting where Max-Pressure is strong.

    Training Depth

    MAPPO currently trains for 30 episodes; learning trend is improving but not yet converged.

    Demand Coverage

    Current setup is vehicle-only and does not yet include bus, pedestrian, or transit interactions.

    Reward Evolution
    Time-loss Only 8434 s
    Multi-objective 7101 s
    Max-Pressure 5659 s
    Switching to a multi-objective reward substantially improved MAPPO stability.
    No controller currently reaches the proposal fairness target of Jain index 0.8.
    Interim conclusion: ASCE is operational, but still below Max-Pressure in this setting.
  6. Remaining Work to Full Scope

    Path from interim benchmark to full TANGO system

    Next milestones follow directly from the proposal and interim report gap analysis.

    Step 1

    Implement PIRA

    Train the planned message-passing GNN surrogate on scenario-outcome pairs.

    Step 2

    Expand Scenarios

    Add lane closures, construction impacts, transit changes, and major-event demand shifts.

    Step 3

    Multimodal Inputs

    Extend beyond car-only flows to include transit, pedestrian, and bike effects where possible.

    Step 4

    Stronger ASCE Training

    Use longer training, curriculum over irregular demand, and imitation warm-start from Max-Pressure.

    Step 5

    Robustness Tests

    Evaluate distribution shift, sensor dropout, and unseen infrastructure scenario generalization.

    Step 6

    Final Delivery

    Report delay, throughput, fairness, and scenario summaries for reproducible final evaluation.
